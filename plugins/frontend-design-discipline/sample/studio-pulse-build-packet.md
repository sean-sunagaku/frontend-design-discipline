# Studio Pulse Build Packet Example

## First Impression

A nocturnal release room, not a generic B2B dashboard.

## Visual Thesis

Dark editorial surfaces, one signal-coral accent, and a precise release workspace that feels observed rather than decorated.

## Anti-Goals

- no left-text/right-mockup hero unless the visual side is overwhelmingly dominant
- no feature card grid in the first impression or in the support section
- no decorative gradients used in place of a believable product surface
- no rainbow dashboard language or synthetic KPI theater
- no copy-heavy sections that explain what the composition should already imply
- no dual-CTA close that feels indecisive

## Section Jobs

### Hero

- job: establish that Studio Pulse is a focused release room
- takeaway: launch decisions can feel calm and exact

### Support

- job: show scattered tools collapsing into one operating surface
- takeaway: fragmentation becomes one reading flow

### Detail

- job: reveal the rhythm of collect, resolve, release
- takeaway: the product sharpens judgment instead of adding speed theater

### Final CTA

- job: close with calm conviction
- takeaway: this is for teams that want clarity over noise

## Dominant Visual Plan

- hero: one large cropped release workspace with annotations and rails
- support: a convergence field where outside tools visibly collapse into one central plane
- detail: a long release rail with editorial callouts, not stacked cards
- final cta: mostly typography and one deliberate action

## Typography and Color Discipline

- typefaces: one clean sans plus one supporting sans only if needed
- accent colors: one signal coral maximum
- contrast: high contrast in the hero, softer but still readable neutrals below
- hierarchy: product name and main promise dominate before supporting copy
- font stance: the display face should feel editorial and precise, not like a generic startup default

## Hero Scale Budget

- desktop headline should hold at roughly 3 lines, not balloon into a wall of type
- the hero visual plane should stay louder than the headline block as a whole
- brand, support copy, and CTA must all breathe in the first viewport
- if reducing the headline by one step improves the composition, the original size was too large

## Section Text Line Budget

- support heading: 2 to 3 lines maximum
- support body: 3 to 4 lines maximum at the chosen measure
- proof-point titles: 1 to 2 lines maximum
- proof-point body: 1 to 2 lines maximum
- detail heading: 2 to 3 lines maximum
- detail body: 2 to 4 lines maximum
- final CTA headline: 2 to 3 lines maximum
- CTA labels and nav labels: 1 line only

If a lower section only works when several neighboring blocks all wrap similarly, rewrite the copy or widen the measure instead.

## Imagery Strategy

Use believable product-surface imagery with strong cropping and calm tonal zones for text. Avoid abstract textures that do not narrate workflow.

## CTA Stance

The close should feel like joining a calmer operating mode, not clicking a growth-hack signup button.

## Pass Bar

- first viewport looks distinct in one glance
- hero feels like one composed poster
- hero headline feels controlled rather than oversized
- lower-section text wraps feel intentional rather than container-driven
- support and detail sections do not collapse into generic blocks
- copy is short enough that removing 30 percent would not materially improve it
- one accent color carries signal without becoming decoration
- the final CTA feels inevitable, not optional
